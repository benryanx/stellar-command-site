THE SHATTERED VEIL UNIVERSE

Before the Shattering,
the Builders made the Veil.

Thousands of years ago, a civilisation known only as the Builders created a transit network spanning the galaxy — a dimension-adjacent layer called the Veil. Through it, ships could cross light-years in seconds. Humanity found their ruins, reverse-engineered their technology, and built an age of expansion. Then something went wrong.

ORIGIN
THE BUILDERS
No one knows what the Builders were, or where they went. What remains are ruins — structures built from a metamaterial that absorbs and transmits Veil harmonic energy in ways human science cannot fully replicate. Every major faction has funded expeditions to Builder sites. Every faction has classified what they found. The ships that incorporate recovered Builder components are the most dangerous vessels in known space — and the least understood.
THE TRANSIT NETWORK
THE VEIL
The Veil is not empty space. Navigators describe it as a place with geometry that does not match the physical universe — where distances compress, where information seems to propagate before it is transmitted, and where something moves in the deep layers that does not respond to hailing frequencies. The Builders built the Veil for a purpose no one has determined. Humanity uses it anyway.
THE CATASTROPHE
THE SHATTERING
The Shattering was not a war, not a natural disaster, and not an accident — or at least, no single explanation has survived scrutiny. In the span of weeks, Veil transit routes destabilised across the galaxy. Supply lines collapsed. Colonial fleets lost contact with home systems. Civilisations that had grown dependent on instant transit found themselves isolated overnight. Two centuries later, the cause remains officially undetermined.
THE UNKNOWN
THE WRAITHS
Since the Shattering, reports have accumulated of entities encountered in Veil-space during transit — shapes that do not match any known vessel design, that move without propulsion signatures, that appear on sensors as nothing consistent with matter. They have been named the Wraiths. They have never been successfully communicated with. Ships carrying Builder metamaterial report increased Wraith activity near their approach vectors. The Builders knew what the Wraiths were. They left no explanation.
THE AGE OF LANTERNS
"Before the Shattering, every colony had a beacon — a signal that said we are here, we are alive, come find us. When the Veil fractured, the beacons kept broadcasting. No one came. That is why they call it the Age of Lanterns. Not because of the light. Because of what the light meant after the darkness."

Stellar Command is set in the aftermath. Centuries after the Shattering, humanity has rebuilt — not into a single civilisation, but into competing factions, each with a different answer to the same question: what do we do now? You play as an independent commander operating in the spaces between them, building your own legacy in a galaxy that is still, slowly, coming back to life.

MAJOR POWERS

Four factions. One fractured galaxy.

Each faction survived the Shattering differently and rebuilt around a different philosophy. As an independent commander, you operate in the spaces between them — trading with some, raiding others, always answerable only to yourself.

⊕
CONCORD OF SOL
MILITARY ORDER · INNER SYSTEMS
The remnant of humanity's pre-Shattering central government, rebuilt around military doctrine and the principle that order must be enforced before it can be chosen. Their ships are the most powerful in known space. Their methods are not questioned by anyone who wants to keep flying.
◎
FREEWAKE COALITION
FRONTIER ALLIANCE · OUTER RIM
Born in the outer rim colonies that the Concord abandoned after the Shattering, the Freewake Coalition is less a government than a promise — that the frontier will protect itself, that independent pilots matter, and that the inner systems do not get to decide how the rim survives.
✕
BLACK BANNER SYNDICATE
CRIMINAL EMPIRE · SHADOW LANES
In the chaos after the Shattering, the Syndicate filled the voids that legitimate governance could not reach. They are not ideological. They have contracts, leverage, and a fleet roster that Concord intelligence has never fully accounted for.
◆
CARTOGRAPHER HOUSES
KNOWLEDGE GUILD · ALL SECTORS
The Cartographer Houses sell one thing: information. Maps of Veil-stable routes. Lore recovered from Builder sites. Intelligence on faction movements. In a galaxy where the Shattering deleted the common knowledge base, whoever controls information controls the future.
GAMEPLAY

A living galaxy. Real stakes.

Stellar Command runs in your browser with no download, no install, no client. Every planet, every beacon, every named system exists in a shared persistent world alongside every other commander online.

⚡
REAL-TIME COMBAT
Pilot your ship directly with mouse and keyboard. Engage enemies with precision lasers, seeker missiles, and timed boost manoeuvres. Every shot is physics-simulated. Every kill is permanent until you respawn.
◎
PLANET ECONOMY
Explore the sector and claim unclaimed planets. Name them — AI generates unique in-universe lore on the spot — and they generate passive Cosmic Token income every second. Other commanders can conquer them back.
▲
FLEET COMMAND
Purchase escort ships and build a fleet that fights alongside you. Unlock advanced fleet skills to boost their firepower, health, and attack range. Your fleet persists across sessions.
⬡
SKILL TREE
Earn a skill point with every rank-up. Spend them across five branches — Combat, Navigation, Industry, Survival, Command — each permanently changing how your ship performs.
◆
ASTEROID MINING
Rich asteroids across the sector contain recoverable loot. Mine them, fill your cargo hold, then sell for Cosmic Tokens. Find an epic Builder Relic Core and a single run funds an entire ship upgrade.
